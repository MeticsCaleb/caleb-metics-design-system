## House defaults (Caleb / Metics Media — these override anything below)

This channel leads with full-frame motion graphics made for complete beginners who are looking to learn new skills or engage with step by step guides, not talking-head overlays. Apply these as the governing defaults; where they conflict with anything later in this document, these win.

- Full-frame first. SOLID full-frame graphics on a white/light base are the DEFAULT clip type. Use ALPHA talking-head overlays only at beats explicitly marked talking-head (typically the open and the CTA).
- No reserved presenter zone. Do not keep the center cells or the bottom-right corner clear for the presenter. Graphics use the entire frame, content centered. A picture-in-picture of the presenter, when wanted, is added manually by the editor as a circle in the top-right — never plan layout around it.
- Default skin: white/light, clean and professional, no grain or vignette. Accent green #1F9E5B, navy #003952 for ink and hero numbers, Manrope display + JetBrains Mono for chips/URLs. (Per-video skins still swap color/font/mood/motifs to match the product being reviewed.)
- Lower-third: "Caleb" with no trailing period; wordmark set as two words, "Metics Media."
- Link / CTA card: a white watch-card that pops up from the bottom, full width, covering the bottom quarter of the frame (light-mode YouTube styling, link in YouTube's light-mode blue). It can hold through the outro crossfade.
- Keep it calm: one idea per slide, icon-and-number led, centered, never fast-paced.

- Keep everything clean and crisp. Clean dark drop shadows — use solid rounded "bubble"/capsule containers and tasteful gradients for depth and separation instead when things are appearing on-screen using an alpha channel and not on top of a solid background.
- On-screen links and small callouts always sit inside a clean rounded bubble/capsule, never as bare drop-shadowed text floating over the footage.
- Never render guides in the output — no rule-of-thirds, safe-zone, or PiP overlays. Those are review-only and must not appear in the final frames.
- Center compositions precisely; if it's centered and clean, that's the bar.
- Type: favor bold, confident weights for display and hero text where the skin allows.
- Utilize clean, minimal design, ideally with icons or clever design language when possible. These videos are made for complete beginners who may feel overwhelmed by complex ideas, large techy language, and some viewers may not even be native English speakers.
- You have creative freedom to deviate from these guidelines occasionally if you have an idea or design that may work better depending on the project. If I don't like it I'll let you know and we'll revise it later. 

Everything else in this document — the technical render contract in animation-kit/, the asset library, the iconography tiers, the affiliate offer-type table — stays exactly as written.
